Here's a time line of Steve Reich's life:

  • 1936 – Born in NYC
  • 1943 – Starts to take music lessons
  • 1957 – Graduates Cornell University with B.A. of philosophy
  • 1963 – Earns master's degree after attending Mills College
  • 1965 – Composes It's gonna rain, composition based on playing the same phrase from two tapes simultaneously, and eventually making them play out of phase
  • 1971 – Finishes his first major piece after five-week trip to Ghana, which vastly influenced Reich's musical style, especially his sense of rhythm
  • 1976 – Premieres the opus magnum work Music for 18 Musicians which turned out to be his the most harmonically complex work till that time
  • 1988 – Tries to pay a homage to the holocaust victims with Different Trains which eventually earned him a Grammy
  • 1993 – Experiments with new means of expression and writes The Cave, a so-called video-opera with his wife Beryl Korot
  • 2011 – Writes WTC 9/11, a composition with similar instrumentation to that of Different Trains
  • 2015 – Tries to break down the wall between “high culture music” and popular music with Radio Rewrite, composition based on music of alternative rock band Radiohead

“My musical tastes are a little unusual.”
